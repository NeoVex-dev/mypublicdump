###### **#Chosing a Game Engine**



Before I started developing the game, I had to choose a game engine that suited my project. Initially I was thinking about programming the game in three.js, inspired by Bruno Simon's portfolio website, although I later realized that it was out of my project scope to learn an entirely new programming language. I decided to stick with a game engine, my options were the big three:

* [Godot](https://godotengine.org/)
* [Unity](https://unity.com/)
* [Unreal Engine](https://www.unrealengine.com/)	

Out of these, the only engine I could sort of run was Godot. Regardless of the low learning curve, I didn't seem to like the developmental process in Godot, and the engine's 3D capabilities weren't what I was aiming for the ending of Elements: Discovery. My remaining options were Unity and Unreal.



They had come pretty close, but Unreal Engine's [Blueprints](https://dev.epicgames.com/documentation/unreal-engine/blueprints-visual-scripting-in-unreal-engine?lang=en-US) brought it to the top. To be honest, I probably should've chosen Unity because I was planning on basing my game in 2D, but I do not enjoy long scripts of coding. As much as I hate to admit it, I only enjoy coding and programming when it is in a puzzle-like scenario, hence my appeal towards Capture-The-Flag exercises.



Due to this, I wanted to find a method to make good games without long scripts, and unreal engine solved this problem natively with Blueprints. The only problem with unreal engine was its lack of support for 2D, so I had to figure that out. After doing some research I came across the [Paper 2D](https://dev.epicgames.com/documentation/unreal-engine/paper-2d-overview-in-unreal-engine?lang=en-US) plugin, it seemed perfect but it lacked many features that are core to engines like unity, eventually i came across the [Paper ZD ](https://www.fab.com/listings/6664e3b5-e376-47aa-a0dd-f7bbbd5b93c0?lang=en)plugin, and after that I had the feeling it was going to work out.



Although my workflow may not be perfect for the game I'm making, I've decided to use Unreal Engine with the paper ZD plugin, and I feel pretty happy about it. However, I'll have to wait for until I have the right setup to run Unreal Engine and see how it turns out, so until then I'll have to keep project momentum by building my game conceptually and documenting it. Before I end, I want to clear one thing up cause I'm not sure how clear I was about it until now. Most of the game would be in a Top-down 2D view, until the ending where the world becomes 3D. For specific scenes the game may shift perspectives to platformer or isometric, although it remains 2D until the ending. Bye for now.



##### ***Decision:* Unreal Engine + PaperZD**

##### 

##### ***Reason:*** <b>Blueprint scripting and long-term flexibility.</b>
